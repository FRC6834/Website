About Us

About Team 6834.

Who are we?


Our team is made up from the staff and students from WMHS. We are divided into different categories starting with leadership, and working down into different groups such as: Build, Programming & Controls, Management & Media, Corporate Sponsorship/Liaison, and work on our Community Impact.

What is our mission?


The goal of the Watkins Bionic Warriors is to bring more science and robotics into our community, and to introduce it to our students to show how it will impact and improve our community, creating high-quality work. To open the mind of adolescents and challenge them to their greatest potential.

What are the benefits?


In the end our team will have gained experience and essential skills in: moral leadership, programming, business, and construction of robotics.
We will properly teach our local community and students more about the potential benefits and abundant opportunities that come from these academic fields. Students find inspiration to pursue STEM careers.

What is FRC?


FIRST (For Inspiration and Recognition of Science and Technology) was founded by inventor Dean Kamen to inspire young people's interest and participation in science and technology.
FIRST is a 501(c) (3) not-for-profit, public charity based in Manchester, N.H. FIRST is volunteer-driven and built on partnerships with individuals, businesses, educational institutions, and government.
Some of the world's most respected companies provide funding, mentorship time, talent, and equipment to make FIRST's mission a reality.

The Robotics team meets during the entire school year. Prior to build season in January, members learn technical skills such as engineering design (CAD) ; trade specific skills in mechanical, electrical, gearing, manipulators, drive systems, and pneumatics systems; programming; project management; and business management.
The culminating event is the FIRST Robotics Competition which starts with the build season. The robot will have to complete tasks such as throwing balls into goals, flying discs into goals, place inner tubes onto racks, hanging on bars, and balancing robots on balance beams. The game for the competition, along with the required set of tasks, changes annually.
